<Section icon={Target} title="Core Progression and Mechanics">

In Wizard Alchemy, progression is tied to your ability to master alchemy and combat. Unlike standard RPGs where you simply level up, here you must actively collect ingredients to brew the power you need. Following a structured path will save you hours of aimless wandering.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Race">
    Start by evaluating your initial race. Use free codes to reroll if you get a low-tier race. Look for races that offer mana regeneration or damage buffs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Gather Basic Materials">
    Explore the starting island to collect common herbs and minerals. These are essential for basic health and mana potions that keep you alive during early fights.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Brew and Experiment">
    Use the alchemy station to combine materials. Refer to the unofficial Wiki for recipes to avoid wasting rare ingredients on failed experiments.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Gear Up">
    Visit the shops to buy your first Wand and Wizard Hat. These items provide flat stat increases that are necessary for tackling the first few bosses.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Join a Party">
    As enemies get tougher, solo play becomes inefficient. Use the Discord to find a group for farming higher-level islands.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Progression Tip">
Focus on unlocking a strong "Race" early. A high-tier race can provide passive benefits that make the initial grind significantly faster.
</Callout>

| Gear Type | Primary Stat | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wands** | Spell Power | Offense-focused builds |
| **Wizard Hats** | Mana Pool | Sustained spellcasting |
| **Robes** | Defense/Resist | Survival in boss fights |
| **Potions** | Temporary Buffs | Burst damage and healing |

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Race Tier List and Combat Strategy">

Races in Wizard Alchemy are not just cosmetic; they define your playstyle. Some races are built for tanking hits, while others are "glass cannons" designed to deal massive magical damage from a distance. Since rerolls can be expensive (or require codes), knowing which races to keep is crucial.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="S-Tier Races">
    - **High Mana Regen**
    - Unique active abilities
    - Massive damage multipliers
    - **Example**: Dragonic or Celestial types
  </Card>
  <Card title="A-Tier Races">
    - Solid all-around stats
    - Good defensive passives
    - Reliable for solo play
    - **Example**: Elf or Vampire variants
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

Combat is a mix of spell management and positioning. You cannot simply spam spells; you must manage your mana and use potions strategically. Bosses often have "telegraphed" attacks that you must dodge to survive, making mobility just as important as your damage output.

<Callout type="tip" title="Mana Management">
Always keep a stack of mana potions in your quick-slot. Running out of mana mid-boss fight is the most common cause of death for new players.
</Callout>
