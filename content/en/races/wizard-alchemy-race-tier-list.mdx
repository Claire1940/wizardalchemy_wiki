## D-Tier: The Underperformers

These races, while playable, offer minimal advantages compared to their counterparts. They often come with significant drawbacks or provide buffs that are simply too minor to make a substantial difference.

*   **Human:** Unfortunately, the Human race sits at the bottom of the D-tier. It offers no inherent buffs, bonuses, or unique abilities, making it a pure vanilla option with zero improvements. While a blank slate can appeal to some for role-playing, it puts you at a significant disadvantage in terms of gameplay mechanics.
*   **Elf:** With approximately a 15% drop chance, the Elf race provides a small boost of +3 base movement speed and +10 jump height. While this enhances mobility, it's generally considered weak when stacked against the more powerful attributes offered by other races. It’s decent for getting around, but lacks combat or survival utility.
*   **Tree Spirit:** Also with a 15% chance, the Tree Spirit focuses on survivability. It doubles your HP regeneration speed and grants +5% max HP. However, this comes at the cost of -2 base movement speed, making you noticeably slower. While the regeneration is nice, the mobility penalty and limited HP boost prevent it from climbing higher in the ranks.
*   **Undead:** The Undead race, with a 10% chance, presents an interesting but flawed ability. Upon taking fatal damage, you survive with 1 HP, enter a brief invincibility state, and restore 30% of your max HP, with a 180-second cooldown. The major downside is a substantial -30% max HP, making you incredibly fragile. Despite gaining +10 skill speed, the HP reduction is often too detrimental to compensate.

## C-Tier: Balanced but Unremarkable

Races in the C-tier are generally balanced, offering a mix of minor buffs without any significant drawbacks or standout features. They are decent options but won't provide a competitive edge.

*   **Werewolf:** With a 10% chance to obtain, the Werewolf race offers a more balanced stat distribution. You gain +10% max HP, +5 attack power, +1 movement speed, and +10 jump height. It's an all-around decent choice for players looking for a slight boost in multiple areas, but it doesn't excel in any particular aspect, keeping it firmly in the C-tier.

## B-Tier: Solid Choices with Specific Strengths

The B-tier is where races start to become genuinely interesting, offering strong advantages that can significantly impact gameplay, especially when paired with the right build or playstyle.

*   **Ice Crystal:** This race, with a 5% chance, is excellent for ice elemental builds. It boosts your ice elemental damage by +20% and provides +10% max HP. The trade-off is a -2 movement speed penalty. If you're focusing on ice magic, the damage boost can be substantial, making it a strong contender in its niche.
*   **Fiendish Demon:** Also with a 5% chance, the Fiendish Demon is the fire-aligned counterpart to the Ice Crystal. It grants +20% fire elemental damage and +2 movement speed, but at the cost of -10% max HP. This is a high-risk, high-reward option, perfect for aggressive players who prioritize offensive power and mobility over tankiness.
*   **Death Eater:** With a 10% chance, the Death Eater is a fantastic choice for sustained combat. It restores 5% max HP whenever you defeat an enemy and provides an additional +10% attack power. The drawback is a halved HP regeneration speed. This race is ideal for players who frequently engage in combat and can consistently secure kills to maintain their health.

## A-Tier: Excellent All-Rounders

A-tier races are incredibly strong and versatile, offering significant benefits that can elevate almost any playstyle. They are highly sought after for their powerful abilities.

*   **Stellar Ambassador:** With a 4% chance, the Stellar Ambassador is an exceptional choice for offensive players. Every time you deal damage, you gain a burst of movement speed, making you incredibly mobile in combat. Additionally, it provides +20 skill speed and +10% attack power, making it a potent all-around offensive race. Its ability to maintain mobility while dishing out damage makes it a top-tier pick for many. This race truly shines in dynamic encounters.

## S-Tier: The Overpowered Dominators

The S-tier represents the absolute best races in *Wizard Alchemy*, offering unparalleled power and unique advantages that can define your gameplay. These are the races you should aim for if you want to maximize your potential.

*   **Festrals:** With a mere 1% chance to obtain, the Festrals race is currently considered the most overpowered option in *Wizard Alchemy*. It comes loaded with a plethora of strong abilities. You gain an exclusive attack effect that applies a "disaster effect" dealing damage over time for 5 seconds whenever you deal damage. Furthermore, your attacks receive +20% dark elemental damage and are converted into dark attribute, making them incredibly potent. On top of that, you get +3% movement speed, +10% jump height, +10% attack power, and a massive +30 skill speed. The sheer volume and quality of these bonuses make Festrals an undisputed S-tier race, offering a significant advantage in any combat scenario. If you're serious about climbing the ranks, the Festrals should be your ultimate goal in this **Wizard Alchemy race tier list**.
