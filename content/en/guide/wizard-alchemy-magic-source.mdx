<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Potion Crafting: Your Magic Source Guide">
The potion brewing system in Wizard Alchemy, while not overly complex, requires strategic material selection to maximize your chances of success. Every material you gather possesses a **Magic Score**, and each potion you wish to craft has a specific **Magic Value** requirement. Your goal is to combine materials whose collective Magic Score meets or exceeds the potion's Magic Value.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Gather Materials">
    Defeat enemies, open chests, and collect resources from the environment (e.g., Blueberry Bushes, Bird Nests, Mushroom Clusters) to acquire various materials. Each material has a unique Magic Power.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Access the Alchemy Menu">
    Navigate to the Alchemy/Brewing menu where you have five slots to place your chosen materials.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Calculate Total Magic Score">
    Sum the Magic Scores of all materials placed in the crafting slots. This total needs to meet or exceed the desired potion's Magic Value.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Incorporate Elemental Shards (Optional but Recommended)">
    For specific elemental spells, add an Elemental Shard (Ice, Fire, Earth, Dark, Light, Wind) corresponding to the spell's element. These shards boost the crafting chance for that element, even though they have a 0 Magic Score themselves.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Execute Quick-Time Events">
    During the crafting process, engage in quick-time events. Your accuracy in these events directly impacts the potion's Purity, which in turn influences its effectiveness (e.g., damage, duration).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Crafting Nuance">
Overshooting a potion's required Magic Value can sometimes lower your chance to craft that specific potion, as you might start qualifying for higher-tier potions instead. Aim for the optimal range.
</Callout>

</Section>
