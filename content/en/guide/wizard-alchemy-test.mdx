<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Brewing Mastery: Passing the Purity Test">

The heart of the game lies in the **Refinery**. When you brew a potion, you enter a mini-game that serves as the ultimate **wizard alchemy test**. Your performance here determines the **Purity** of the resulting potion. Purity is not just a cosmetic stat; it directly scales the damage, range, and cooldown of the spell you unlock.

Potions are categorized into tiers based on their purity and the rarity of the ingredients used. Mixing multiple potions of the same type in the Refinery can result in an upgraded version with significantly higher stats.

</CardGrid>

Drinking a potion with low purity will replace your current skill with a weaker version. Always check the **Purity Power %** before clicking "Use" on a new brew.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Zap} title="Progression Path: Leveling and Ascension">

Progression in **Wizard Alchemy** is gated by a system called **Ascension**. You cannot simply grind to level 100 in one sitting. Instead, you hit a "cap" that requires gold and a specific level to break. Each ascension increases your level cap, provides permanent stat boosts, and changes your title—from a lowly Junior Apprentice to a Master Wizard.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The First Cap">
    Reach **Level 10** by farming Forest Bandits and Dwarves. Complete the repeatable quests from the NPCs in the starter village to accumulate the gold needed for your first ascension.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Ascending to Apprentice">
    Open the stats menu and select **Ascend**. Your level will reset to 1, but your max level increases to 20. You will also gain access to better **Wizard Robes** that provide HP boosts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Advanced Brewing">
    At Level 20, you should move to the **Magic Source Beach**. Use rare materials like **Seagull Eggs** and **Magic Bones** to craft offensive spells like Fire Arrow or Meteorite.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Gear Optimization">
    Invest your gold into the **Ember Staff** or the **Demonic Trident**. These weapons provide a higher base attack boost, making it easier to one-tap mobs for faster XP.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="XP Strategy">
Using abilities grants XP even if you don't hit a mob. If you are close to a level up, spam your low-cooldown spells while running between quest objectives to maximize efficiency.
</Callout>

| Ascension Title | Level Req | Gold Cost | Max Level Cap |

| **Junior Apprentice** | 10 | 500 | 20 |

<Section icon={Shield} title="Races and Stat Allocation">

Your race is a fundamental part of your build, providing passive buffs that can define your playstyle. When you start the game, you are given a random race, but you can use **Race Rerolls** to try for something better. Races like the **Ice Crystal** or **Stellar Ambassador** are highly coveted for their elemental mastery and damage multipliers.

When allocating stats, most players focus entirely on **Damage**. While this allows for "glass cannon" builds that can one-tap bosses, you may find yourself struggling with high-damage archers later on.

<Callout type="info" title="Race Rarity">
Legendary races have a drop rate of less than 1%. If you roll a race with a **10% Power boost** early on, keep it until you have accumulated at least 20+ rerolls to ensure you don't end up with a worse "Human" or "Tree Spirit" race.
</Callout>
