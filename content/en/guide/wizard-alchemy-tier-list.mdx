<Section icon={Shield} title="A-Tier Races: Strong Alternatives for Diverse Builds">
While Thestrals might be the dream, several A-tier races offer excellent benefits and can carry you through most of **Wizard Alchemy**'s content. These races cater to different playstyles, from high-burst damage to sustained farming.

### Stellar Ambassador (4% Roll)
This race is a fantastic choice for high-DPS builds that value mobility. When you deal damage, you gain a burst of movement speed, which is incredibly useful for gap closing or kiting dangerous enemies and bosses.

| Ability                | Benefit                                            | Use Case                                   |
| :--------------------- | :------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------- |
| Movement Speed Burst   | Great for gap closing and kiting                   | Fast-paced combat, dodging boss mechanics  |
| +20 Skill Speed        | Faster spell casting and ability usage             | Maximizing DPS rotations                   |
| +10% Attack Power      | General damage boost, not element-locked           | Versatile for any spell element            |

<Callout type="info">
Stellar Ambassador is a beginner-friendly option as its benefits are not tied to a specific elemental spell type, making it adaptable to various early game builds.
</Callout>

### Death Eater (10% Roll)
For players who prefer solo farming and require strong sustain, Death Eater is an exceptional A-tier choice. Its unique healing mechanic allows for extended grinding sessions without needing to constantly retreat or use potions.

| Ability                      | Benefit                                      | Drawback                     |
| :--------------------------- | :------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------- |
| Restores 5% Max HP on kill   | Incredible sustain for solo play             | Halved HP regeneration speed |
| +10% Attack Power            | General damage boost, not element-locked     | None                         |

<Callout type="tip">
Death Eater excels in open-world farming and content where you face numerous weaker enemies. The constant healing ensures you remain topped off, making grinding safer and more efficient in **Wizard Alchemy**.
</Callout>

### Conditional A-Tier: Fiendish Demon & Ice Crystal
These two races are powerful but require specific elemental builds to reach their full potential. They offer significant damage boosts for their respective elements but come with trade-offs.

<Section icon={Map} title="How to Get Race Rerolls in Wizard Alchemy">
To optimize your character in **Wizard Alchemy** and land an S-tier or A-tier race, you'll need Race Rerolls. There are a few ways to acquire these, ensuring you have ample opportunities to customize your magical lineage.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Redeem Active Codes">
    The developers frequently release codes that grant free Race Rerolls. These are often announced on official social media channels and Discord. As of May 2026, the following codes are active:
    - **`RELEASE`**: Grants 5 Race Rerolls
    - **`WIZARD`**: Grants 5 Race Rerolls
    - **`MACHI`**: Grants 5 Race Rerolls
    Redeem these codes via the in-game Settings menu under "Utilities" by typing them into the designated box.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Purchase with Robux">
    If you're looking for more rerolls beyond the free codes, you can purchase them directly from the in-game shop using Robux. This is a quick way to get multiple rerolls if you're struggling to land your desired race.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Reroll Process">
    Once you have Race Rerolls, navigate to the Stats > Race > Race Menu section in your game menu. Here, you'll find the option to "Reroll" your current race, consuming one reroll per attempt.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Important Reroll Advice">
Your starting race in **Wizard Alchemy** is Human, which provides no bonuses. It is crucial to reroll Human immediately. After that, always check the tier list before spending more rerolls. If you land Thestrals (1% rarity), stop. Any A-tier race is also a great stopping point, with Werewolf (B-tier) being a comfortable early stop.
</Callout>

### Werewolf (10% Roll)
Werewolf is a solid B-tier option, especially for beginners. It offers a balanced set of stat boosts, making it a good all-rounder for early game progression.

| Stat Boost             | Value | Benefit                               |
| :--------------------- | :---- | :------------------------------------ |

| Attack Power           | +5    | Small damage increase                 |
| Base Movement Speed    | +1    | Minor mobility boost                  |
| Jump Height            | +10   | Enhanced vertical movement            |

<Callout type="info">
Werewolf is a decent early game race that provides a bit of everything. While it gets outscaled by higher-tier races, it's a perfectly acceptable option to keep if you're short on rerolls and haven't landed an A or S-tier race yet.
</Callout>
